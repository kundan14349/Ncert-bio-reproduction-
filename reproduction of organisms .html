Chapter: Reproduction in Organisms

Notes

1. Reproduction: Reproduction is the biological process by which new individuals of the same species are produced.

2. Types of Reproduction:

  • Asexual Reproduction: Involves a single parent and produces offspring that are genetically identical to the parent.
  • Sexual Reproduction: Involves two parents and produces offspring that are genetically diverse.

3. Asexual Reproduction Methods:

  • Binary Fission (e.g., Amoeba)
  • Budding (e.g., Hydra)
  • Spore Formation (e.g., Fungi)
  • Vegetative Propagation (e.g., Plants)

4. Sexual Reproduction in Plants:

  • Flowers are the reproductive structures in plants.
  • Male part: Stamen (Anther and Filament)
  • Female part: Pistil (Stigma, Style, and Ovary)
